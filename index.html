About Me

I'm a Ph.D. researcher in Computer Science at Drexel University, working under the supervision of Dr. Ali Shokoufandeh. My work sits at the intersection of generative modeling, deep learning, and mathematical structure, with a focus on understanding and designing learning systems not just as empirical tools, but as operators‚Äîobjects with stability, geometry, and theoretical guarantees.

My current research explores transformer architectures and generative models through a theoretical and systems-oriented lens. Particularly, my research is driven by a single organizing question: how should we design learning operators that are simultaneously expressive, stable, and aligned with the geometry of the data they act on? I approach generative modeling as a problem of operator design, studying how diffusion-based dynamics and attention mechanisms shape information flow, stability, and spectral behavior. My goal is to bridge ideas from operator theory, physics-informed modeling, and modern generative AI, including diffusion and attention-based models.

Beyond theory, I enjoy building systems that work in practice. My projects span applications from materials generation to social media analysis and computer vision in healthcare. Across these domains, I care deeply about models that are robust, interpretable, and principled, not just accurate.

I earned my B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from the University of Tehran, graduating with a 4.0 GPA and a concentration in machine learning and AI. Since then, I've worked across academia and industry, developing end-to-end ML pipelines, publishing peer-reviewed research, and collaborating across disciplines.

Ongoing Projects

Current research projects I'm actively working on, spanning attention mechanisms, graph neural networks, and AI for materials science.

Under Review @ ICLR 2026

Fourier-Based Attention

Developing a learnable Fourier-based attention mechanism that enables frequency-aware context modeling. This approach advances positional encoding for transformers, achieving +1.8pp improvement over Roformer by leveraging spectral properties of input sequences.

In Progress

Spectral Attention Mechanism

Designing a transformer architecture that regularizes attention before softmax via an SPD resolvent on queries and keys with layerwise damping. This produces spectrally controlled, stable attention with bounded gradients, addressing training instabilities in deep transformers.

In Progress

Oscillatory Graph Neural Networks

Building a GNN architecture where nodes act as damped harmonic oscillators with adaptive, state-dependent coupling. This framework comes with proven phase-locked synchronization properties, providing theoretical guarantees for message passing dynamics.

In Progress

MXene Materials Generation

Collaborating with the Chemical Engineering Department on a grammar-constrained Transformer for generating valid MXene materials. Using reinforcement learning to enforce chemical validity constraints, achieving 97.7% valid outputs for novel material discovery.
